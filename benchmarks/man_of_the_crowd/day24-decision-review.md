# Day-24 Decision Review

## Decision

**Continue to an author-facing pilot, while keeping visual generation human-reviewed.**

The benchmark proves that the current research repository can represent one literary adaptation as structured, source-linked, provider-neutral production data and carry it through panel planning, visual creation, continuity validation, approval, and reproducible export.

It does not prove product-market fit, commercial unit economics, or automatic long-form consistency. Those require author/reader validation and repeated chapters.

## Visual QA

The storyboard was reviewed at original resolution against the approved visual direction.

Passed:

- exactly eight panels in a two-column by four-row portrait layout;
- no captions, speech balloons, logos, or watermark;
- narrator remains visually distinct from the old man;
- old man's facial identity, age, body type, and ragged clothing remain consistent;
- coffeehouse, gas lamps, rain, crowd, and period styling support 1840 London;
- progression reads from observation to recognition to pursuit;
- diamond and dagger are reserved for the final detail panel.

Accepted prototype limitations:

- the storyboard is benchmark concept art, not publication-ready sequential art;
- exact architecture is atmospheric rather than a documented reconstruction of one London street;
- emotional nuance still depends on human art direction;
- a single composite sheet tests within-page consistency, not consistency across many independently generated assets.

## Rights Review

- Source story is public domain.
- The benchmark is for research, product validation, and portfolio demonstration.
- Modern copyrighted stories must not enter production without written adaptation permission.
- A future author agreement must define formats, territory, term, exclusivity, revenue share, AI use, training use, approvals, and termination.

## Cost Review

No OpenAI API key or paid API call was used by the local application. The visual storyboard was produced through the built-in image-generation capability. Provider-specific API pricing and per-chapter unit economics remain intentionally unclaimed.

Future pilots must record:

- attempts per approved panel;
- human editing minutes;
- generation and storage cost;
- author revision rounds;
- cost per publishable chapter.

## Next Validation Gate

Before building animation or a public marketplace:

1. Interview 3–5 independent authors.
2. Secure one narrowly scoped written pilot permission.
3. Produce one author-controlled illustrated chapter.
4. Compare it with text-only reading using at least 10 readers.
5. Record willingness to pay, license, revise, publish, or share.
6. Repeat generation across separate assets to measure cross-image consistency.

## Go/No-Go Standard

Continue when at least one author commits meaningful time or rights to a controlled pilot, readers understand and value the format, and human correction remains economically plausible.

Change the wedge if authors value the output but will not pay or grant limited rights. Pause expansion if continuity requires full traditional-studio labor or readers receive no meaningful benefit.
